Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity is the concept that a person's brain is malleable. The brain is able to modify and adapt in response to experience and stimulus. Understanding and embracing this mindset is beneficial as it helps people better learn and engage with material.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Understanding that the brain is able to modify itself to better accommodate learning something new can help me overcome fear that 'I might not be cut out for this type of work'. Seeing challenges and roadblocks in learning as 'when' not 'if' problems and believing that through practice and repetition I am able to master new things will help me immensely.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

A lot of the concepts we have explored so far during Foundations are good ways to increase neuroplasticity. For example, finding clarity, setting goals, practicing mindfulness, altering your mindset and getting into a routine are all recommended ways to give yourself a leg up on your neuroplastic learning journey.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

A Growth Mindset can be described as a way of viewing challenges in learning something new as 'when' not 'if' problems. It is the mindset that with enough practice, effort and conviction anything can be learned and mastered. A fixed mindset is the belief that 'this is too hard', or 'I might not be cut out for this', the belief they may not be able to achieve their goals. A growth mindset is relevant to our Dev Academy learning journey as JavaScript and coding is foreign to a lot of us, and can be very overwhelming at times. Keeping in mind that the struggle is temporary and that we can overcome it with enough practice and effort is vital.
